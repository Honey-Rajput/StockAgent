# 📖 VDU Scanner - Strategy & Tab Guide

Welcome to the comprehensive guide for the VDU Scanner application. This tool contains multiple distinct scanning strategies separated into tabs. Below is a detailed explanation of what each tab does and how you can use it to find high-probability trade setups.

---

## 🏗️ Core Scanners

### 📊 Results (VDU Breakouts)
**What it checks:** Scans for "Volume Dry-Up" (VDU) breakouts. It looks for stocks that have had a quiet consolidation period with extremely low volume, followed by a sudden price surge on massive volume today.
**How to use:** This is your primary tab for explosive swing trades. Look for stocks with high "Signal Strength" and a Volume Ratio of >2x.

### 🎯 VCP + Minervini
**What it checks:** Combines Mark Minervini's Long-Term Trend Template (stock must be in a confirmed uptrend) with a Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP). It looks for decreasing price volatility and volume leading up to a breakout point.
**How to use:** Extremely powerful for finding safe entry points in established bull trends. Buy exactly when the stock breaks above the tight contraction (the "pivot").

### 🏆 Minervini (Trend Template)
**What it checks:** Strictly scans for Mark Minervini's Trend Template. Ensures the stock is trading above its 50, 150, and 200 moving averages, and that the averages are stacked sequentially.
**How to use:** Use this to filter out the noise. If a stock doesn't pass this scan, it is not in a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend.

### 📉 Dan Zanger Scanner
**What it checks:** Inspired by Dan Zanger, this scans for explosive momentum breakouts accompanied by abnormally high volume (often 300%+ of average) out of chart patterns like bull flags or channels.
**How to use:** Best for aggressive traders looking for multi-day/multi-week massive momentum runners.

## 📈 Moving Average & Trend Scanners

### 📈 20 & 50 SMA
**What it checks:** Finds "Quality Stocks" that are respecting their 20-day and 50-day Simple Moving Averages across Daily, Weekly, and Monthly timeframes. 
**How to use:** Great for finding smooth, steady uptrends. Buy near the 20 SMA; exit if it breaks below the 50 SMA.

### 🛡️ 65 SMA
**What it checks:** Specifically targets the 65-day SMA, which is a powerful institutional support level often respected by large funds during market pullbacks.
**How to use:** Look for stocks touching or slightly piercing the 65 SMA and forming a bullish reversal candle.

### 📈 9/21 EMA Support
**What it checks:** Looks for very short-term momentum pullbacks. It finds stocks in strong trends that are briefly pulling back to touch their 9-day or 21-day Exponential Moving Averages.
**How to use:** Excellent for entering fast-moving momentum stocks on the first or second pullback. 

### 🔄 MA Cross
**What it checks:** Scans for moving average crossovers (e.g., the 20-day crossing above the 50-day, commonly known as a golden cross).
**How to use:** A classic trend-following signal indicating a shift in momentum from bearish to bullish.

## 🎯 Reversal & Oversold Scanners

### 🎯 RSI Oversold
**What it checks:** Finds stocks where the 14-day RSI has dropped into deep oversold territory (typically ≤ 35). 
**How to use:** The ultimate mean-reversion scanner. Wait for a green confirmation candle before buying to catch the "rubber band" snap-back effect.

### 🛡️ Support Bounce
**What it checks:** Identifies stocks that are currently sitting at a historical support level where they have previously bounced multiple times.
**How to use:** Buy at the support line with a very tight stop loss just below it. High reward-to-risk ratio.

### 🌊 Wave (WaveTrend)
**What it checks:** Uses the WaveTrend oscillator to find extreme oversold conditions (WT < -40) and flags stocks when the fast wave crosses above the slow wave (a green dot buy signal).
**How to use:** Excellent for timing the exact bottom of a pullback.

## 🧠 Advanced & Institutional Analytics

### 🚥 VPA (Volume Price Analysis)
**What it checks:** Analyzes the relationship between candle spread (high to low) and volume using Wyckoff principles. It detects anomalies like "stopping volume" (high volume but price stops falling) or "no demand" (price rises on low volume).
**How to use:** Use this to read the footprints of institutional buyers and sellers. 

### 🚀 Stage 2 Breakout & 🏆 Stage Analysis
**What it checks:** Based on Stan Weinstein's theories, it categorizes stocks into Stage 1 (Base), Stage 2 (Uptrend), Stage 3 (Top), or Stage 4 (Decline). 
**How to use:** Only trade stocks breaking from Stage 1 into Stage 2. Avoid Stage 3 and 4 entirely.

### 📊 Vol Profile
**What it checks:** Analyzes volume executed at specific price levels rather than times, identifying the "Point of Control" (where the most volume was traded).
**How to use:** Price will naturally gravitate towards or bounce off the Point of Control. Use it as a magnet for take-profit targets or entry supports.

### 💎 Confluence
**What it checks:** Aggregates multiple indicators (SMA, RSI, MACD, Volume) to find stocks where multiple technical signals are firing "Buy" at the exact same time.
**How to use:** The ultimate high-conviction tab. The more signals align, the higher the probability of the trade.
